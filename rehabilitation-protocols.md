# 🏋️ Rehabilitation Protocols — ACL Recovery in Female Athletes

> Extracted and structured from clinical trial intervention arms registered on ClinicalTrials.gov.  
> For clinical reference use. Always apply protocols under qualified supervision.

## Protocol 1 — Neurostimulation-Augmented Quadriceps Rehabilitation
**Source Trial:** NCT07128602 | Arcadia University (NIH-funded) | Status: Recruiting

### Overview
A 6-session protocol combining **anodal transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS)** over the primary motor cortex with isolated quadriceps strengthening on an isokinetic dynamometer. Designed exclusively for **women 2–6 months post-ACLR**.

### Rationale
Post-ACLR quadriceps weakness is partly neurological: reduced corticospinal excitability (CSE) limits the brain's ability to fully activate thigh muscles. tDCS boosts CSE non-invasively. This deficit is more pronounced in females than males.

### Protocol Details

| Parameter | Active tDCS Group | Sham tDCS Group |
|-----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Stimulation Duration** | 20 minutes | 30 sec on / 30 sec off (sham) |
| **Electrode Placement** | Anode over primary motor cortex (contralateral to surgical limb) | Same placement, no sustained current |
| **Concurrent Exercise** | Quadriceps torque matching task on isokinetic dynamometer | Same exercise |
| **Sessions** | 6 total | 6 total |
| **Blinding** | Triple-blind (participant, provider, investigator) | — |

### Outcome Measures
- Peak torque (MVIC, 5 sec) — pre/post each session
- Rate of Torque Development (RTD): 0–100ms, 100–200ms
- Corticospinal Excitability: Active Motor Threshold (AMT) + TMS recruitment curve slope
- Torque steadiness

### Clinical Takeaway
> This protocol is experimental and not yet standard of care. However, it represents a paradigm shift: treating the **neural** component of quad weakness, not just the muscular component. Sports medicine teams should monitor trial results (expected ~2028) for potential integration into post-ACLR pathways for female athletes.

## Protocol 2 — Plyometric + Hamstring Strengthening Prevention Program
**Source Trial:** NCT07047833 | University of Lahore | Status: Completed (2025)

### Overview
A **6-week, twice-weekly** injury prevention training program for female amateur athletes (ages 18–30) in jumping/cutting sports. Compares combined plyometric + hamstring strengthening vs. plyometric training alone.

### Rationale
Female athletes have a 2–8x higher ACL injury risk than males, partly due to hamstring-to-quadriceps strength imbalances and poor neuromuscular control during landing. Hamstring strengthening may address this imbalance.

### Protocol Details

#### Group A — Plyometric + Hamstring Strengthening (60 min/session)
| Component | Exercises | Duration |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Warm-Up** | Dynamic stretching, light jogging | 15 min |
| **Plyometric Training** | Squat jumps, depth jumps, lateral jumps (progressive intensity) | 35 min |
| **Hamstring Strengthening** | Nordic hamstring curls, machine-based leg curls | 10–15 min |
| **Cool-Down** | Static stretching, breathing | 5 min |

#### Group B — Plyometric Only (40 min/session)
| Component | Exercises | Duration |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Warm-Up** | Dynamic stretching, light jogging | 15 min |
| **Plyometric Training** | Squat jumps, depth jumps, lateral jumps (progressive intensity) | 20 min |
| **Cool-Down** | Static stretching | 5 min |

### Assessment Schedule
- **Baseline**, **Week 3**, **Week 6**
- Tools: Handheld dynamometer (strength), Star Excursion Balance Test (SEBT), KOOS questionnaire

### Clinical Takeaway
> This protocol is **immediately implementable** in sports medicine and athletic training settings. The plyometric component is well-established; the hamstring strengthening addition (Nordic curls in particular) is supported by growing evidence for ACL injury prevention. Suitable for pre-season conditioning programs for female soccer, basketball, and volleyball athletes.

## Protocol 3 — VR Mindfulness Meditation + Advanced Neuromuscular Training
**Source Trial:** NCT05527171 | UNC Chapel Hill (NIH/NIAMS-funded) | Status: Recruiting

### Overview
An **8-week psychological + physical** rehabilitation program for women 6 months to 10 years post-ACLR who have kinesiophobia (injury-related fear) and poor jump-landing mechanics. Combines VR mindfulness with advanced neuromuscular training.

### Rationale
Injury-related fear (kinesiophobia) is a primary barrier to return-to-sport and is associated with poor movement patterns and increased re-injury risk. Standard rehab does not address this psychological component. VR mindfulness has been shown to be more effective than traditional mindfulness at inducing mindful states.

### Protocol Details

#### Intervention Group — VR Mindfulness + Advanced Neuromuscular Training
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 3x per week × 8 weeks |
| **VR Mindfulness** | Guided mindfulness meditation in immersive virtual environment (participant choice of setting) |
| **Mindfulness Focus** | Sustained attention on body, breath, sensations; non-reactive awareness of thoughts/feelings |
| **Neuromuscular Training** | Advanced program (details per standard ACLR return-to-sport protocols) |

#### Control Group — VR Sham + Advanced Neuromuscular Training
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 3x per week × 8 weeks |
| **VR Sham** | Immersive virtual environment (beach, forest, lake) without guided meditation |
| **Neuromuscular Training** | Same advanced program as intervention group |

### Assessment Tools
| Outcome | Tool |
|---------|------|
| Injury-related fear | Tampa Scale of Kinesiophobia-11 (TSK-11; range 11–44) |
| Jump-landing biomechanics | 3D motion capture + force plates (knee abduction moment, flexion excursion) |
| Brain activity | 3T fMRI (BOLD signal in Default Mode Network during sport-specific image task) |
| Feasibility | Recruitment %, retention %, acceptability survey |

### Clinical Takeaway
> This protocol highlights the importance of **psychological readiness** in ACL return-to-sport. Even if not yet using VR, clinicians should screen for kinesiophobia (TSK-11 ≥19 = elevated fear) and incorporate mindfulness or psychological skills training into rehab plans for female athletes. VR systems may become accessible tools within the next 2–3 years pending trial results.

## Protocol 4 — Blood Flow Restriction (BFR) ± Total Motion Release (TMR)
**Source Trial:** NCT07589309 | Green International University | Status: Completed (2026)

### Overview
A **6-month, three-sessions-per-week** post-ACLR rehabilitation protocol comparing BFR training alone vs. BFR combined with Total Motion Release (TMR) — a contralateral movement symmetry technique. For adults ages 16–45.

### Rationale
BFR training allows muscle hypertrophy and strength gains using low loads (20–30% 1RM), reducing stress on healing tissue. TMR addresses neuromuscular movement asymmetries by treating the non-painful side to induce bilateral neuromotor improvements.

### Protocol Details

#### Group A — BFR + TMR (Experimental)
| Phase | Component | Details |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Pre-Strengthening** | TMR Exercises | Trunk rotations, side bending, hip shifts — performed on **contralateral (non-painful) side** |
| **Strengthening** | BFR Training | Inflatable cuff at **60–80% limb occlusion pressure** (proximal thigh) |
| **BFR Exercises** | Low-load resistance | Mini squats, knee extensions |
| **Frequency** | 3 sessions/week | 6 months total |

#### Group B — BFR Only (Active Comparator)
| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **BFR Training** | Same cuff, same pressure (60–80% occlusion) |
| **Exercises** | Mini squats, knee extensions (same as Group A) |
| **No TMR** | Standard BFR + traditional physical therapy only |

### Assessment Schedule
- **Baseline**, **6 weeks**, **12 weeks**, **6 months**
- Tools: Hand-held dynamometer (isometric strength), Single Leg Stance (SLS) test, IKDC Subjective Knee Form (0–100), Limb Symmetry Index (LSI)

### Clinical Takeaway
> BFR training is now well-supported for early post-ACLR rehab and is increasingly available in clinical settings. The addition of TMR is a novel concept worth monitoring. The **Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) ≥90%** benchmark from this trial aligns with contemporary return-to-sport criteria. Results expected in late 2026.

## Protocol 5 — Oxandrolone Pharmacological Adjuvant (IMOX Study)
**Source Trial:** NCT06974526 | Science Valley Research Institute | Status: Recruiting (Phase II)

### Overview
A **24-week pharmacological adjuvant protocol** using a bioabsorbable subdermal oxandrolone implant alongside standard post-ACLR rehabilitation. Phase II safety/tolerability trial with exploratory efficacy endpoints.

### Rationale
Muscle atrophy after ACLR is a major contributor to quadriceps weakness and delayed return-to-sport. Oxandrolone (DHT-derived anabolic steroid; 13:1 anabolic:androgenic ratio) promotes muscle mass and strength recovery with fewer virilizing side effects than other androgens.

### Protocol Details

| Parameter | Oxandrolone Group | Placebo Group |
|-----------|------------------|---------------|
| **Female dose** | 200mg subdermal implant (1 pellet) | Matching placebo implant |
| **Male dose** | 400mg subdermal implant (2 pellets) | Matching placebo implant |
| **Delivery** | Single subdermal insertion at randomization | Same procedure |
| **Duration** | 24 weeks | 24 weeks |
| **Background Rehab** | Standard post-ACLR physiotherapy (all participants) | Same |

### Female-Specific Safety Monitoring
| Parameter | Assessment Timing |
|-----------|------------------|
| Hirsutism (Ferriman-Gallwey score) | Pre-insertion, 4w, 12w, 24w |
| Alopecia | Pre-insertion, 4w, 12w, 24w |
| Acne (physician-assessed) | Pre-insertion, 4w, 12w, 24w |
| Voice deepening (self-report) | Pre-insertion, 4w, 12w, 24w |
| Hormonal profile (FSH, LH, testosterone) | Pre-insertion, 4w, 12w, 24w |

### Efficacy Endpoints (Exploratory)
| Outcome | Tool |
|---------|------|
| Muscle mass | MRI thigh volume + bioimpedance |
| Strength | Isometric dynamometry (peak torque, LSI) |
| Functional ability | Lysholm Knee Score, Y-Balance Test |
| Quality of life | SF-36 |
| Mental health | GAD-7 (anxiety), PHQ-9 (depression) |

### Clinical Takeaway
> This protocol is **experimental and not currently recommended outside clinical trial settings**. However, it represents an important frontier: pharmacological support for muscle recovery in ACL rehab. Sports medicine teams should be aware of this approach, especially for female athletes experiencing prolonged muscle atrophy post-ACLR. Monitor IMOX trial results (expected 2027).
