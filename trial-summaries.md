## Trial 1 — NCT07128602
### Non-Invasive Brain Stimulation (tDCS) to Improve Quadriceps Function After ACL Reconstruction

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT07128602 |
| **Status** | 🟢 RECRUITING |
| **Phase** | N/A (Interventional Device Trial) |
| **Sponsor** | Arcadia University |
| **NIH Funding** | 1R15HD118518-01 (NICHD) |
| **PI** | Ryan Zarzycki, PT, PhD (Associate Professor) |
| **Start Date** | June 6, 2025 |
| **Est. Completion** | March 1, 2028 |
| **Enrollment** | 42 (estimated) |
| **Population** | Females only, ages 18–35, 2–6 months post-ACLR |
| **Location** | Glenside, Pennsylvania, USA |

#### 🎯 Objective
To determine whether anodal **transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS)** — a non-invasive brain stimulation technique — can increase corticospinal excitability (CSE) and thereby improve quadriceps muscle performance in women recovering from ACL reconstruction. Background evidence shows that females have more severe quadriceps deficits and lower neural drive post-ACLR than males, yet current rehab protocols do not target neural drive directly.

#### 🔬 Design
- **Type:** Randomized, triple-blinded, parallel-group RCT
- **Arms:** Active tDCS vs. Sham tDCS (20 min over primary motor cortex during quad exercises)
- **Sessions:** 6 sessions on isokinetic dynamometer

#### 📏 Primary Outcomes
1. **Peak quadriceps torque** — maximal voluntary isometric contraction (MVIC) pre/post
2. **Corticospinal excitability (slope)** — TMS-induced stimulus-response curve

#### 📏 Secondary Outcomes
- Rate of Torque Development (RTD) at 0–100ms and 100–200ms
- Active Motor Threshold (AMT) via TMS

#### 💡 Key Findings / Clinical Relevance
> *Study currently recruiting — no results yet.* This trial directly addresses the female-specific neural deficit gap in ACL rehab. If successful, tDCS could become a standard adjunct in post-ACLR physical therapy for women, targeting the brain-to-muscle signaling pathway that conventional strength training does not reach.

## Trial 2 — NCT07047833
### Plyometric Training With or Without Hamstring Strengthening for Preventing ACL Injuries in Female Amateur Athletes

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT07047833 |
| **Status** | ✅ COMPLETED |
| **Phase** | N/A (Interventional Behavioral) |
| **Sponsor** | University of Lahore, Pakistan |
| **PI** | Fareeha Raja |
| **Start Date** | May 21, 2024 |
| **Completion Date** | March 25, 2025 |
| **Enrollment** | 60 (actual) |
| **Population** | Female amateur athletes, ages 18–30 |
| **Location** | University of Lahore Teaching Hospital, Lahore, Pakistan |

#### 🎯 Objective
To evaluate whether adding **targeted hamstring strengthening** (Nordic curls + machine leg curls) to a **plyometric training** program provides superior benefits over plyometric training alone in preventing ACL injuries in female amateur athletes engaged in jumping/cutting sports (basketball, soccer).

#### 🔬 Design
- **Type:** Randomized controlled trial, single-blind (outcomes assessor blinded)
- **Arm A:** Plyometric + Hamstring Strengthening (60 min/session, 2x/week, 6 weeks)
- **Arm B:** Plyometric Only (40 min/session, 2x/week, 6 weeks)
- **Assessments:** Baseline, Week 3, Week 6

#### 📏 Primary Outcomes
1. **Knee flexion and extension strength** — handheld dynamometer (kg)

#### 📏 Secondary Outcomes
- **Dynamic balance** — Star Excursion Balance Test (SEBT)
- **Functional mobility** — KOOS (Knee Injury and Osteoarthritis Outcome Score)

#### 💡 Key Findings / Clinical Relevance
> *Completed March 2025 — results pending publication.* This is one of the first RCTs to directly compare combined plyometric + hamstring strengthening against plyometric-only training **exclusively in female amateur athletes**. The KOOS and SEBT outcomes are particularly valuable for return-to-sport decision-making. The 6-week, twice-weekly structure is highly practical for real-world sports medicine settings.

## Trial 3 — NCT05527171
### Virtual Reality Mindfulness Meditation After ACL Reconstruction

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT05527171 |
| **Status** | 🟢 RECRUITING |
| **Phase** | N/A (Interventional Behavioral) |
| **Sponsor** | University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill |
| **NIH Funding** | K23AR079056 (NIAMS) |
| **PI** | Shelby E. Baez, PhD, ATC |
| **Start Date** | September 5, 2023 |
| **Est. Completion** | June 30, 2026 |
| **Enrollment** | 48 (estimated) |
| **Population** | Females only, ages 14–25, 6 months–10 years post-ACLR with kinesiophobia |
| **Location** | Chapel Hill, North Carolina, USA |

#### 🎯 Objective
To evaluate whether **Virtual Reality (VR) Mindfulness Meditation** (3x/week for 8 weeks) combined with advanced neuromuscular training can:
1. Decrease **injury-related fear** (kinesiophobia)
2. Improve **jump-landing biomechanics** (knee abduction moment, flexion excursion)
3. Improve **brain activity** (fMRI BOLD signal in Default Mode Network)

...compared to VR sham + the same neuromuscular training in women post-ACLR.

#### 🔬 Design
- **Type:** Randomized, double-blind (participant + outcomes assessor), parallel RCT
- **Arm A:** VR Mindfulness Meditation + Advanced Neuromuscular Training
- **Arm B:** VR Sham (virtual environment, no meditation) + Advanced Neuromuscular Training
- **Duration:** 8 weeks, 3 sessions/week

#### 📏 Primary Outcomes
1. Feasibility (recruitment %, retention %)
2. Acceptability survey score (target: mean >8)
3. **Tampa Scale of Kinesiophobia-11** (injury-related fear; range 11–44)

#### 📏 Secondary Outcomes
- External knee abduction moment (biomechanics via 3D motion capture)
- Knee flexion excursion (jump-landing task)
- Mean % BOLD fMRI signal in Default Mode Network

#### 💡 Key Findings / Clinical Relevance
> *Currently recruiting.* This is a pioneering trial addressing the **psychological and neurological dimensions** of ACL recovery in women — a critically underserved area. Kinesiophobia is a major barrier to return-to-sport and is associated with increased re-injury risk. The use of VR makes this intervention scalable and clinician-friendly. Data expected mid-2026.

## Trial 4 — NCT07589309
### Blood Flow Restriction (BFR) With or Without Total Motion Release (TMR) in ACL Rehabilitation

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT07589309 |
| **Acronym** | BFR-TMR-ACL |
| **Status** | ✅ COMPLETED |
| **Phase** | N/A (Interventional, Device + Procedure) |
| **Sponsor** | Green International University, Pakistan |
| **Collaborator** | Sir Ganga Ram Hospital |
| **PI** | Ali Muslim Raza |
| **Start Date** | November 2, 2025 |
| **Completion Date** | May 5, 2026 |
| **Enrollment** | 44 (actual) |
| **Population** | Males and females, ages 16–45, 2–4 weeks post-ACLR |
| **Location** | Lahore, Punjab, Pakistan |

#### 🎯 Objective
To compare **BFR + TMR** (combined) vs. **BFR alone** in restoring muscular strength, balance, and knee functionality following primary unilateral ACL reconstruction. TMR targets movement asymmetries on the contralateral (non-painful) side to indirectly improve the injured side.

#### 🔬 Design
- **Type:** Randomized, triple-blind (participant, care provider, outcomes assessor), parallel RCT
- **Arm A (Experimental):** BFR (60–80% occlusion, mini squats + knee extensions) + TMR (trunk rotations, hip shifts, contralateral side)
- **Arm B (Control):** BFR only
- **Duration:** 6 months, 3 sessions/week
- **Assessments:** Baseline, 6 weeks, 12 weeks, 6 months

#### 📏 Primary Outcomes
1. **Maximum isometric lower limb strength** — handheld dynamometer
2. **Single Leg Stance (SLS) Test** — balance and proprioception duration
3. **IKDC Subjective Knee Form** — functional disability score (0–100, higher = better)

#### 💡 Key Findings / Clinical Relevance
> *Completed May 2026 — results pending publication.* This is one of the most practically impactful recent ACL rehab trials, directly comparing two evidence-based modalities. The addition of TMR (a movement symmetry technique) to BFR represents a novel integrative approach. The 6-month follow-up and use of Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) make findings directly applicable to return-to-sport clearance criteria.

## Trial 5 — NCT06974526
### Subdermal Oxandrolone Implant as Adjuvant Treatment in ACL Rehabilitation (IMOX Study)

| Field | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **NCT ID** | NCT06974526 |
| **Acronym** | IMOX |
| **Status** | 🟢 RECRUITING |
| **Phase** | Phase II |
| **Sponsor** | Science Valley Research Institute (Brazil) |
| **Collaborator** | Stin Pharma |
| **PI** | Roberto Tauchmann, MD |
| **Study Chair** | André Malavasi, MD, PhD |
| **Start Date** | November 28, 2025 |
| **Est. Completion** | April 30, 2027 |
| **Enrollment** | 96 (estimated) |
| **Population** | Males and females, ages 18–60, post-ACLR with hamstring autograft |
| **Locations** | 6 sites across Brazil (Fortaleza, Belo Horizonte, Curitiba, Brusque, Santo André, Santos) |

#### 🎯 Objective
To evaluate the **safety, tolerability, and exploratory efficacy** of a **bioabsorbable subdermal oxandrolone implant** (anabolic steroid with 13:1 anabolic-to-androgenic ratio) as an adjuvant to standard rehabilitation following ACL reconstruction, over 24 weeks vs. placebo implant.

#### 🔬 Design
- **Type:** Phase II, randomized, quadruple-blind (participant, care provider, investigator, outcomes assessor), multicenter, parallel, placebo-controlled
- **Arm A:** Oxandrolone implant (women: 200mg; men: 400mg, subdermal)
- **Arm B:** Placebo implant (matching)
- **Duration:** 24 weeks

#### 📏 Primary Outcomes
1. **Composite of serious adverse events (SAEs)** related to treatment at 24 weeks

#### 📏 Secondary Outcomes (female-specific monitoring)
- **Androgenization signs:** hirsutism (Ferriman-Gallwey score), alopecia, acne, voice deepening (self-report)
- Hormonal profile: testosterone (total/free), FSH, LH
- Biochemical, metabolic, hemostasis profiles

#### 📏 Exploratory Efficacy Outcomes
- Muscle mass recovery (MRI thigh volume, bioimpedance)
- Isometric strength (quadriceps peak torque, Limb Symmetry Index)
- Functional outcomes: Lysholm Knee Score, Y-Balance Test, SF-36 QoL
- Psychological: GAD-7 (anxiety), PHQ-9 (depression), Patient Acceptable Symptom State

#### ⚠️ Female-Specific Considerations
Women receive a lower dose (200mg vs. 400mg for men). The study protocol includes rigorous monitoring for androgenization. Exclusion criteria include pregnancy, PCOS, suspected breast carcinoma, and Grade 5 acne or significant pre-existing hirsutism.

#### 💡 Key Findings / Clinical Relevance
> *Currently recruiting at 6 Brazilian sites.* This is the **only Phase II pharmacological trial** in the current ACL rehab landscape targeting muscle recovery acceleration. The subdermal delivery route is novel and may overcome oral administration issues. Results will be critical for determining whether anabolic support is a viable, safe option for female athletes post-ACLR.
